Q&A

List three main differences between web1, web2 and web3
The first differnet is interaction: web1: User interaction is minimal. There are no mechanisms for users to contribute or modify content. Information flow is one-way, from the website to the user. web2: Interaction is high. Users can comment, share, like, and engage with content and with each other. Social media, blogs, and forums are typical of this era. web3: Interaction is enhanced and decentralized. Users interact directly with decentralized applications (dApps) and each other without relying on a central authority. The second different is Infrastructure: web1:Centralized servers host static content. Websites are individually managed, with content delivery happening from a central server to the end-user. web2:Centralized platforms with powerful databases and content management systems (CMS) deliver dynamic content. Services are hosted on large-scale centralized data centers. web3:Decentralized networks and distributed ledger technology replace central servers. Data is stored across a distributed network, ensuring security, transparency, and user control. The third different is User Experience: web1:User experience is limited and passive. Users primarily consume content without interacting or contributing. Navigation is straightforward but lacks personalization. web2:User experience is interactive and collaborative. Websites are designed to engage users through comments, likes, shares, and personalized feeds. web3: User experience is personalized and secure. Users interact with decentralized applications that provide tailored experiences based on AI and semantic understanding.
Explain the differences stated in question 1 by an example of a use case
In Web1, a query for "Side Effects of Medicines" would lead to a static page hosted on a centralized server, providing fixed information with minimal interaction. Users can only read the content without any means to comment, share, or ask questions. For example, a simple HTML page from a pharmaceutical company listing side effects offers no interactivity or personalization. In contrast, Web2 provides a more dynamic and interactive experience. The same query would yield a Wikipedia page or a YouTube video with user comments and discussions. Users can contribute by commenting, liking, and sharing information, creating a collaborative environment. This content is delivered through centralized platforms with powerful databases, making the experience interactive and personalized based on user interactions. Moving to Web3, the query would produce context-aware, personalized results on a decentralized platform. Users interact directly with decentralized applications (dApps) without relying on a central authority. For instance, a decentralized health platform might use blockchain to verify and secure user-contributed data about medication side effects. Here, an AI-powered assistant could provide tailored recommendations based on the user's health profile. The infrastructure relies on decentralized networks and distributed ledger technology, ensuring security, transparency, and user control over their data, offering a highly personalized and secure user experience..
